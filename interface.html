Let's Understand the interface

DaVinci Resolve is a professional video editing software known for its powerful features, including advanced color grading, video editing, audio post-production, and visual effects. The interface is designed to accommodate a wide range of tasks and offers a user-friendly layout that is customizable to fit different workflows.

  1. Media Pool

    Located in the upper left corner, this is where all your media assets (video clips, images, audio files, etc.) are stored and organized. You can import files directly here, and it also provides the option to create bins (folders) to organize your media.

  2. Timeline

    The Timeline is the central area where you arrange and edit your video clips. It’s found at the bottom of the interface and is divided into video tracks and audio tracks. You can drag media from the Media Pool to the Timeline to start assembling your project. It’s where you cut, trim, and layer clips.

  3. Inspector

    Located in the upper right, the Inspector panel allows you to adjust the properties of selected clips. You can modify things like position, scale, rotation, audio levels, and color corrections directly from here. It’s an essential tool for fine-tuning edits.

  4. Video Preview/Viewer

    This is where you view your clips and edits. You can watch the video playback in real-time or frame-by-frame. The viewer is located in the upper center of the interface and allows you to see exactly what’s happening on your timeline. It also has controls for zooming and adjusting the view.

  5. Cut Page

    This is one of the primary workspaces in DaVinci Resolve and is optimized for quick editing. It provides a streamlined interface with a timeline at the bottom, media pool at the top, and additional editing tools on the right. It's great for quick trimming, rough cuts, and organizing clips.

  6. Edit Page

    The Edit Page is the main workspace for traditional video editing tasks. Here, you have access to advanced tools like trimming, transitions, keyframing, multi-cam editing, and more. This is the go-to page for most editing work.

  7. Fusion Page

    The Fusion Page is for visual effects and compositing. It offers a node-based workspace where you can create intricate effects and transitions. It is used for motion graphics, keying, tracking, and compositing.

  8. Color Page

    As the name suggests, the Color Page is dedicated to color grading and correction. You can adjust exposure, contrast, color balance, and apply LUTs (Look-Up Tables) for creative color grading. DaVinci Resolve is known for its powerful and precise color grading tools, which are widely used in professional film production.

  9. Fairlight

    The Fairlight Page is dedicated to audio post-production. It provides tools for mixing, editing, and enhancing audio. Here, you can adjust audio levels, apply filters, synchronize sound, and perform tasks like noise reduction and mastering.

  10. Deliver Page

    The Deliver Page is where you export your final project. It allows you to choose from various export settings, including resolution, file format, codecs, and audio settings. You can create delivery presets, such as for YouTube, Vimeo, or custom outputs, to simplify the export process.

  11. Toolbars and Menus

    The top bar contains essential options such as File, Edit, View, and Window. It includes tools for saving projects, undo/redo actions, importing/exporting media, and accessing project settings.
